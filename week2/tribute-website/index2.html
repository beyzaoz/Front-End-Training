"If coincidence hadn't brought you to my path, I would have lived the same way,

but without knowing anything. You taught me that there is another kind of life in the world,

and that I also have a soul."

-Sabahattin Ali

Born on February 25, 1907, in Eğridere, Gümülcine (located within today's Bulgarian borders), Sabahattin Ali,
spent his childhood in various cities across Anatolia due to his father’s military service.
Following his education at the Balıkesir and Istanbul Teachers' Colleges, he began his professional career as a teacher in Yozgat.
In 1928, he was sent to Germany by the Ministry of National Education, where he deepened his knowledge of the German language and European culture for two years.
Upon his return, he taught in cities like Aydın and Konya. During this period, he solidified his literary identity and
penned his first short stories, focusing on the life of the Anatolian people and their social issues.

During the peak of his literary career, he became one of the most important representatives of the social realist movement in Turkish literature with novels
considered cornerstones of the genre, such as *Kuyucaklı Yusuf* (1937), *The Devil Inside Us* (*İçimizdeki Şeytan*, 1940) and especially *Madonna in a Fur Coat* (*Kürk Mantolu Madonna*, 1943).
In his novels and short stories, he masterfully explored the drama of ordinary people, social injustice, and the individual's inner
alienation. However, due to the critical tone and political stance in his works,
he faced oppression even while working in government institutions and was imprisoned in the 1930s on charges of insulting Atatürk.
Towards the late 1940s, his writings in political satire magazines, such as *Markopaşa*,
intensified the lawsuits and political pressure against him.

As the pressure became unbearable and his opportunities for stable employment diminished, the author, in 1948,
decided to flee the country. During his attempt to cross the border into Bulgaria,
he was tragically murdered near Kırklareli on April 2, 1948,
by Ali Ertekin, the guide he had hired. His short life, which ended at the age of 41,
left behind a legacy of timeless works in Turkish literature, distinguished by their depth and social sensitivity.
Due to his tragic end,
Sabahattin Ali remains one of the most mournful and impactful figures
in Turkish literary history.
