>A Lost Day in Tokyo: Between Silence and Noise

The moment I stepped into Tokyo, I felt the city pull me in. On one side, neon lights flowing through crowds; on the other, the calm of ancient temples. In the morning, I was lost in the human tide at Shibuya Crossing, and by the afternoon, I was walking through the quiet forest paths of Meiji Shrine, hearing nothing but birds. When evening came, I found myself in a ramen bar, watching the city’s chaos while warming up with a hot bowl of ramen. Tokyo is one of those rare cities that lets you feel both chaos and peace at the same time.

Finding the Soul in Bali: Ubud’s Green Embrace

Before my trip, everyone told me “Bali is another world,” and they were right. Walking through Ubud’s rice terraces makes you feel like time has slowed down. After morning yoga, I enjoyed a breakfast of fresh tropical fruits, met curious monkeys at the Monkey Forest, and watched the sky turn golden along Campuhan Ridge Walk at sunset. Bali isn’t just a vacation—it's a gentle reset for the soul.
